Family Planning:Empowering Families, Building Futures

  1. Hormonal Methods
    2. These include birth control pills, patches, injections (like Depo-Provera), and vaginal rings.
    Hormonal methods work by regulating ovulation and altering the uterine lining to prevent pregnancy.
  2. Barrier Methods
    3. Condoms (male and female), diaphragms, and cervical caps physically block sperm from reaching the egg.
    Condoms are also the only method that provides protection against sexually transmitted infections (STIs), making them a vital option for many.
  3. Long-Acting Reversible Contraceptives (LARCs)
    4. These include intrauterine devices (IUDs) and implants.
    Once placed, they offer long-term protection—ranging from 3 to 10 years—without the need for daily attention.
  4. Natural Family Planning (Fertility Awareness)
    5. By tracking fertility signs like body temperature and cervical mucus, individuals can plan or avoid pregnancy naturally.
  5. Permanent Methods
    6. For those who are certain they do not want children or any more children, sterilization procedures such as tubal ligation for women or vasectomy for men provide a permanent solution.
